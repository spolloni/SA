\begin{frame}
\frametitle{Main Takeaway}
\centering

\begin{itemize}
  \item Previous studies find large air pollution impacts in specific industries, in specific areas, even in specific firms
  \item But air pollution spreads far and wide, so environmental policy needs to be broad (city-level, province-level, country-level)
  \item This research fills this gap: studies all industries over a long-time range
  \item Conclusion: reducing pollution to international standards has little overall benefit to producer surplus 
  \begin{itemize}
    \item 0.26\% compared to 5.6\% for fixing electric shortages
  \end{itemize}
\end{itemize}

\end{frame}



\begin{frame}
\frametitle{Questions and New Directions}

\begin{itemize}

\item \textbf{Other impacts}: Do firms enter/exit/move in response to air pollution? Which types?
  \begin{itemize}
    \item Does pollution impact the variance of productivity shocks and capital investment decisions of firms?
    \item Is there evidence of adaptation over time?
    \item Is there data on labor migration reallocation across space?
  \end{itemize}

\vspace{2mm}

\item \textbf{Policy}: Can your research recommend more targeted policies?
  \begin{itemize}
    \item Are sensitive industries geographically concentrated?
    \item How far does air pollution travel?
  \end{itemize}

\end{itemize}

\end{frame}
